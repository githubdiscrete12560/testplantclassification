Wi‑Fi Security · inside

WPA2 / WPA3
WPA Security Mode

Wi‑Fi Protected Access — the rulebook that controls authentication. It verifies your identity before letting you connect.

  • WPA2‑Personal – most common, uses a pre‑shared password (PSK).
  • WPA3‑Personal – newer, stronger; resists offline dictionary attacks even with weak passwords.
  • Works like a bouncer — checks your ID (password) before entry.
Home / small office PSK (Pre‑Shared Key)
Your router shows WPA2‑Personal (WPA3‑Personal) – it supports both, and will use WPA3 if your device can.
AES Encryption

Advanced Encryption Standard — the cipher that scrambles your data. Even if intercepted, it looks like gibberish.

  • Military‑grade symmetric encryption (used by governments).
  • Uses complex math (Rijndael) to turn readable data into ciphertext.
  • Like a secret language – only your device and router understand it.
128‑bit / 256‑bit CCMP (counter mode)
AES is the only encryption allowed in WPA2‑Personal (mandatory).
